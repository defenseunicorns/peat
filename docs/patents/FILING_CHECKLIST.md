## Filing Process

### Option A: File via USPTO EFS-Web (Recommended)

**Advantages**:
- Electronic filing (faster processing)
- Automatic filing receipt
- Lower cost (no paper filing surcharge)
- Track application status online

**Steps**:

1. **Log in to EFS-Web**: https://efs.uspto.gov/

2. **Start New Application**:
   - Click "Unregistered eFiler" or "Registered eFiler"
   - Select "Utility Patent Application (including Provisional)"
   - Select "Provisional"

3. **Upload Documents** (for EACH application):
   - Application Data Sheet (ADS) PDF
   - Specification PDF (convert .md to PDF)
   - No formal drawings required (included in spec)

4. **Complete Application Information**:
   - Title of invention
   - Applicant information
   - Inventor information
   - Correspondence information

5. **Fee Calculation**:
   - System will calculate filing fee based on entity status
   - Confirm amount

6. **Payment**:
   - Enter payment method (credit card or deposit account)
   - Authorize charges

7. **Review and Submit**:
   - Review all documents
   - Confirm information is correct
   - Click "Submit"

8. **Save Filing Receipt**:
   - Download and save PDF of filing receipt
   - Note application number and filing date
   - Save confirmation number

**Time Estimate**: 30-60 minutes per application

### Option B: File via Patent Attorney

**Advantages**:
- Professional review of documents
- Attorney ensures proper formatting
- Attorney handles technical filing details
- Reduces risk of filing errors

**Steps**:

1. ☐ Send all documents to attorney:
   - Provisional specifications (.md files)
   - Application Data Sheets
   - Inventor information

2. ☐ Attorney reviews and converts to USPTO format

3. ☐ Attorney files via EFS-Web on your behalf

4. ☐ Receive filing receipts from attorney

**Cost Estimate**: $500 - $1,500 per application (attorney fees only)
**Total Cost**: $1,000 - $3,000 + filing fees

### Option C: File by Mail (Not Recommended)

Paper filing requires:
- Print all documents
- Include check for filing fee
- Mail to USPTO address
- Add 2-4 weeks processing time
- $400 surcharge for paper filing

**Not recommended** - EFS-Web is faster, cheaper, and more reliable.

## Questions & Troubleshooting

### Q: Can I file these provisionals myself without an attorney?

**A**: Yes, provisional patents can be filed pro se (without attorney). The provisionals we've prepared are comprehensive and ready to file. However, we **strongly recommend** consulting an attorney for:
- Review of specifications (2-4 hours, $500-$1,000)
- Utility patent strategy planning
- Prior art analysis
- Claim drafting guidance for utility stage

**Compromise**: File provisionals yourself ($130-$260), then consult attorney at month 6 for utility strategy.

### Q: Do I need to file formal patent drawings?

**A**: For provisional patents, NO. Informal drawings (including ASCII art) are acceptable. Our specifications include all necessary figures as ASCII diagrams.

For utility patents, you MAY need formal drawings depending on examiner requirements. Cross that bridge when you get to utility stage.

### Q: What if I make improvements after filing?

**A**: You have two options:
1. **File continuation/CIP** (Continuation-in-Part) that adds new matter
2. **Include in utility patent** (describe improvements when converting to utility)

For now: Document all improvements in engineering logs and ADRs. Decide at utility stage whether to include them.

### Q: Should I file internationally (PCT)?

**A**: Only if:
- Technology has proven commercial value (revenue > $1M+)
- International market is critical (Europe, China, Japan)
- You have funding ($15,000-$35,000+ for PCT + national phases)

**For most startups**: File US provisional now, evaluate international filing at month 9-11 based on traction.

### Q: What about the COD project relationship?

**A**: We've addressed this proactively in Provisional #2:
- Disclosed relationship to COD project
- Documented differentiation (novel features not in COD)
- Confirmed independent development at (r)evolve LLC
- No government rights claimed

**Key**: The features we claim (authority-weighted election, cognitive load, MOS matching) were NOT in COD. This is documented in the application.

### Q: Can I publish or present this work after filing?

**A**: YES! After provisional filing, you can:
- Publish papers
- Give conference talks
- Demo at trade shows
- Discuss with potential customers/investors

The provisional establishes your priority date. Just don't publish BEFORE filing (that creates prior art against yourself).

### Q: What if I miss the 12-month utility deadline?

**A**: You lose priority date. The technology becomes prior art after 18 months. You CAN file a utility patent after that, but:
- No priority claim to provisional
- Prior art cutoff is utility filing date (not provisional date)
- Less patent protection

**Don't miss this deadline!** Set multiple calendar reminders.
